About Me

I am a Health & Safety Practitioner with a background in Mechanical Engineering and diverse experience across construction, power, and remote digital safety. My mission is to transform safety from being a compliance checklist into a culture of collective responsibility.

My Journey

2018–2019 | Mechanical Engineering (Foundation)
Internship at a Refinery Port → first exposure to industrial safety. Inspired by incidents like Piper Alpha. Completed Diploma in Industrial Safety alongside Mechanical Engineering.

2020 | NEBOSH & First Role
Enrolled in NEBOSH IGC, passed in first attempt (Feb 2020). First role as Safety Officer in Thermal Power Plant project. Learned practical safety but role ended due to COVID-19 pandemic.

2021–2023 | Shankar Electricals (Major Growth)
Joined as Safety Engineer, later promoted to Lead Safety managing 180+ workers. Faced critical lifting disputes, enforced compliance under pressure, and received Best Performer Award. Completed IOSH Managing Safely (2021). Led 5 projects simultaneously.

2023–May 2025 | Contract & Remote Roles
Short-term Safety Engineer contract → established safety procedures, assessments, online certifications. Remote safety role → verified AI-captured safety images, performed digital risk assessments.

June 2025 – Present | UK Transition & Learning
Relocated to the UK (13 June 2025). Focused on UK standards (COSHH, HSWA 1974, ISO 45001). Achieved TechIOSH Membership. Completed CPDs in Diploma in Environmental Management, Behaviour-Based Safety, and Environmental Management & Sustainability (UKAF).

Safety Insights

The Growth of Safety

From the unsafe factories of the Industrial Revolution to today’s advanced compliance systems, safety has evolved into a strategic investment for organisations.

Hazards Then vs Now

Then: poor ventilation, unsafe machinery, fire, child labour.
Now: chemical exposure, ergonomics, psychosocial stress, digital fatigue, AI-related risks.

Global Responsibility

In many countries, safety is still treated as optional, leading to preventable tragedies. True progress comes only when safety is recognised as a collective responsibility shared by every worker, supervisor, and employer.
